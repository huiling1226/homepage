I am a PhD student in the Department of Computer Science at Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU), advised by Prof. Jianliang Xu. Before joining HKBU, I received my M.Eng. in Computer Science and Technology and my B.Eng. in Software Engineering from Zhengzhou University in 2024 and 2021, respectively, where I was advised by Prof. Yafei Li during my master's studies.

Research. My research focuses on database systems and spatial data management, with a strong emphasis on designing efficient algorithms and index structures for large-scale data processing. I am particularly interested in:

  • Vector Databases & ANNS — designing highly efficient, disk-based index structures (e.g., monotonic graph indexes) and representative sampling algorithms for large-scale high-dimensional Approximate Nearest Neighbor Search.
  • Spatial Crowdsourcing — developing mobility prediction-aware, fairness-guaranteed task assignment algorithms, and dynamic ridesharing optimization for smart city mobility services.
  • Human-in-the-loop Systems — building visual analysis tools and real-time task allocation systems to bridge human intelligence with complex spatial data management.

My work has been published at top-tier database, AI, and data mining venues including SIGMOD, PVLDB, ICDE, AAAI, and IEEE TMC. I am always open to insightful discussions and research collaborations in the above areas — please feel free to reach out.
